Carcinogenicity, Mutagenicity, Reproductive Toxicity and Sensitization

This section looks at five different health hazards; Carcinogenicity, Mutagenicity, Reproductive Toxicity, Skin Sensitization and Respiratory Sensitization.
The explanation as to why these health hazards are grouped together here can be found in GHS Rev. 10, 2023, where the cut-off concentration limits are uniform across these various hazards.

Carcinogenicity

As defined in GHS Rev. 10, 2023, Carcinogenicity refers to the induction of cancer or an increase in the incidence of cancer occuring after exposure to a substance or mixture. The classification of a mixture as posing a carcinogenic hazard is based on its inherent properties and does not provide information on the level of human cancer risk.

The cut-off concentration limit for any ingredient classified as either a Category 1A, 1B or Category 2 carcinogen is therefore greater or equal to a concentration of 0.1%.

As defined in GHS Rev. 10, 2023, Germ Cell Mutagenicity refers to heritable gene mutations, including heritable structural and numerical chromosome aberrations in germ cells occurring after exposure to a substance or mixture. Reproductive Toxicity

As defined in GHS Rev. 10, 2023, reproductive toxicity is classified based on a multitude of adverse effects on sexual function and fertility in adult males and females, as well as developmental toxicity occuring after exposure to a substance or mixture.

Skin & Respiratory Sensitization

As defined in GHS Rev. 10, 2023, respiratory sensitization refers to hypersensitivity of the airways occuring after inhalation of a substance or a mixture. Skin sensitization refers to an allergic response occuring after skin contact with a substance or a mixture.
